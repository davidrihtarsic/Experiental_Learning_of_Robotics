# ELECTRONICS FUNDAMENTALS

* [Basic Circuit Components](https://davidrihtarsic.github.io/Experiental_Learning_of_Robotics/Learning_Activities/2.11_Debugging_and_testing.html)
  This section introduces resistors, diodes, power sources, and batteries as essential building blocks of electronic circuits. It explains how each component functions, with practical notes on powering an Arduino Uno from USB, external adapters, or LiPo batteries. A UPS power supply and its schematic are also discussed, including resistor-divider voltage control.
  **Keywords:** resistors, diodes, power supply, Arduino Uno, UPS, voltage regulator.

* [Ohm’s Law](https://davidrihtarsic.github.io/Experiental_Learning_of_Robotics/Learning_Activities/2.11_Debugging_and_testing.html)
  Ohm’s Law describes the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance. It is used to calculate current in motors or lamps, ensuring safe power design in robotics. Example calculations show how to compute $I = V/R$ and $P = V \cdot I$ for robotic circuits.
  **Keywords:** Ohm’s Law, current, voltage, resistance, power.
  **Key formulas:** `I = V / R`, `P = V * I`.

* [Kirchhoff’s Current Rule](https://davidrihtarsic.github.io/Experiental_Learning_of_Robotics/Learning_Activities/2.11_Debugging_and_testing.html)
  Kirchhoff’s Current Rule (KCL) states that the total current entering a junction equals the total current leaving it. Practical examples show current distribution in robotic circuits with multiple sensors. Calculations highlight the importance of designing stable power networks.
  **Keywords:** KCL, current conservation, sensors, junction, power distribution.
  **Key formula:** `Σ I_in = Σ I_out`.

* [Kirchhoff’s Voltage Rule](https://davidrihtarsic.github.io/Experiental_Learning_of_Robotics/Learning_Activities/4.4_Kirchhoffs_Voltage_Rule.html)
  Kirchhoff’s Voltage Rule (KVL) ensures that the sum of voltages around a closed loop equals zero. Applied to LED and resistor circuits, it demonstrates energy conservation in robotic devices. Calculations show how to determine voltage across components in a loop.
  **Keywords:** KVL, voltage conservation, LEDs, resistors, loop equation.
  **Key formula:** `Σ U = 0`.

* [Digital Output](https://davidrihtarsic.github.io/Experiental_Learning_of_Robotics/Learning_Activities/4.4_Kirchhoffs_Voltage_Rule.html)
  Digital outputs provide HIGH (5V) or LOW (0V) signals for controlling LEDs, motors, or relays. Using `pinMode(pin, OUTPUT);` defines an output, while `digitalWrite(pin, HIGH);` sets it ON. This section highlights practical uses of digital outputs in robotics.
  **Keywords:** digital output, Arduino, pinMode, digitalWrite, HIGH, LOW.
  **Key commands:** `pinMode()`, `digitalWrite()`.

* [Digital Input](https://davidrihtarsic.github.io/Experiental_Learning_of_Robotics/Learning_Activities/4.4_Kirchhoffs_Voltage_Rule.html)
  Digital inputs read binary states from buttons, touch sensors, or light sensors. `pinMode(pin, INPUT);` defines an input, and `digitalRead(pin)` returns its state. Examples show how push buttons or sensors trigger actions in a robot.
  **Keywords:** digital input, sensors, Arduino, pinMode, digitalRead.
  **Key commands:** `pinMode()`, `digitalRead()`.

* [Move to Reference Position](https://davidrihtarsic.github.io/Experiental_Learning_of_Robotics/Learning_Activities/8.7_Advanced_topics_threading_memory_management_templates.html)
